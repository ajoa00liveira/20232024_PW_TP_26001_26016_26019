Exhibitions of Art

See the most famous attractions in each exhibition in the Art division

Classical and Ancient Art

This exhibition transports visitors back to the ancient times of Greece and Rome, featuring works of art that reflect the beliefs, values ​​and mythologies of these ancient civilizations. From Greek vases adorned with mythological scenes to enigmatic sculptures, the artifacts on display offer a fascinating window into the distant past and the richness of artistic expression of these ancient cultures.

Greek Vase: A Greek ceramic vase from the Classical period, decorated with mythological scenes and intricate patterns, representing the art and culture of Ancient Greece.

Roman Mosaic: A fragment of a Roman mosaic that depicts scenes from everyday Roman life, such as banquets or hunting scenes, demonstrating the skill and technical sophistication of Roman artists.

"The School of Athens" - Raphael: One of the most famous works of the Renaissance, it portrays Greek philosophers such as Plato and Aristotle in a grand architectural environment, symbolizing the intellectual flowering of Antiquity.

Medieval Art

In this exhibition, visitors are immersed in the era of the Middle Ages, where art was inextricably linked to religion and everyday life. Vivid lighting, detailed sculptures, and narrative tapestries transport viewers to a world of knights, castles, and spiritual fervor. Each work reveals the values, beliefs and technical mastery of medieval artists, capturing the spirituality and unique atmosphere of the time.

Medieval Illumination: An illuminated manuscript with detailed illustrations and ornate lettering, representing the art of the Middle Ages and the rich tradition of copyist monks.

Medieval Tapestry: A section of a medieval tapestry, woven with colorful threads and depicting scenes of battle, hunting, or courtly scenes, highlighting the skill of medieval weavers and their storytelling talent.

Medieval Armor and Weapons: A set of weapons and armor used in medieval times.

Sculptures

This exhibition celebrates sculptural tradition over the centuries, highlighting the skill and creativity of sculptors across different eras and styles. From the perfect contours of the Renaissance to the dramatic forms of the Baroque and contemporary experimentation, each work on display reveals artists ability to bring three-dimensional forms to life and express a range of emotions and ideas through sculpture.

"David" - Michelangelo: A replica of a sculpture by Michelangelo, "David" which represents the biblical hero David, from the Old Testament, moments before facing the giant Goliath.

"Ecstasy of Saint Teresa" - Gian Lorenzo Bernini: It is one of Bernini's most famous masterpieces and exemplifies the dramatization characteristic of Baroque sculpture.

"Le Penseur" (The Thinker) - Auguste Rodin: is a sculpture created by Auguste Rodin between 1880 and 1882. The sculpture depicts a moment of deep thought and contemplation, capturing the muscular tension and concentrated expression of the subject.

Modern Art

In this exhibition, visitors are invited to explore the revolutionary artistic movements of the 19th and 20th centuries, which challenged traditional conventions and redefined art. From Picasso's Cubism to provocative Dadaist works and contemporary installations, each work on display offers a unique insight into the social, political and technological changes that have shaped modern art.

"Balloon Flower (Magenta)" - Jeff Koons: One of Koons' best-known works, this giant inflatable flower-shaped sculpture is made from color-coated stainless steel and is emblematic of his pop and provocative aesthetic.

"Maman" - Louise Bourgeois: This giant sculpture of a spider, made of bronze, steel and marble, is a symbolic representation of motherhood and protection, exploring themes of fragility and strength.

"For the Love of God" - Damien Hirst (2007): A human skull sculpture made entirely of platinum and encrusted with 8,601 diamonds, this work by Hirst challenges concepts of mortality, luxury and value in contemporary society.

Painters and their history

This exhibition is a fascinating journey through art history, highlighting the painters and artistic movements that shaped the world of painting. From the Renaissance masters to the Impressionists, each section of the exhibition immerses visitors in the lives, techniques and influences of the most renowned painters of all time.

Leonardo da Vinci was one of the most influential artists of the Italian Renaissance. In addition to his skills as a painter, he was a visionary inventor, scientist, and anatomist. Her most famous work is the "Mona Lisa", known for her enigmatic smile.

Van Gogh was one of the most emblematic artists of post- impressionism. His work is known for its bold brushstrokes and vibrant colors. Despite personal struggles and financial difficulties during his life, he produced some of the most recognized paintings in art history, including "Starry Night", "Sunflowers" and "The Potato Eaters".

Pablo Picasso was one of the most influential artists of the 20th century and one of the founders of cubism. His work spans a wide range of styles and periods, including the Blue period, Rose period and his Cubist phase. Among his most famous works are "Les Demoiselles d'Avignon" and "Guernica"
