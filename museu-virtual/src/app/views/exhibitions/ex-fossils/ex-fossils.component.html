Dinosaur Fossils

The "Dinosaur Fossils" exhibit features a fascinating collection of dinosaur skeletons and fossilized remains. Highlights include the complete skeleton of a Tyrannosaurus Rex, fossilized eggs, etc... This exhibition offers a detailed view of the life, reproduction and behavior of the giants that dominated the Earth during the Mesozoic.

Tyrannosaurus Rex Complete Skeleton: A mounted skeleton of a T. rex, showing its imposing structure and sharp teeth, exemplifying one of the largest predators of the dinosaur era.

Pre-historic Animal Fossils

The "Prehistoric Fossils" exhibit offers a journey into the ancient past, featuring fossils of organisms that inhabited the Earth before and during the age of dinosaurs. This exhibit illustrates the diversity of ancient life and the first steps in the evolution of complex life on the planet.

Trilobite Fossil: A fossil of a trilobite, one of the earliest forms of complex life on Earth, dating to the Cambrian period.

Fossil of a Mammoth: Complete skeleton of a mammoth that existed more than 15 thousand years ago.

Prehistoric Fish Fossil Plate: A sedimentary plate containing fossils of fish that inhabited prehistoric seas, showing the diversity of ancient aquatic life.

Jurassic World

It's in the Jurassic World exhibition that you can see your favorite dinosaurs as if they had never disappeared! Here you will be able to find out more about each Dinosaur and their daily life (at least what is known about it).

Replica of a T-rex: Figure of the T-rex dinosaur in all its splendor, showcasing its formidable teeth, powerful jaws, and massive size, giving visitors a glimpse into the life of one of the most fearsome predators to ever walk the Earth.

Replica of a Brachiosaurus: Life-size Brachiosaurus dinosaur figure, standing tall with its long neck reaching towards the sky, illustrating the grandeur and unique posture of this gentle giant that roamed the Jurassic forests millions of years ago.

Natural history

"Nature World (Animals that are Extinct)" celebrates the memory of species that have disappeared due to climate change and human activity, providing an in-depth look at the lives and extinctions of large historic mammals and birds.

Replica of a dodo: A sculpted and painted replica of the Dodo, an iconic bird that inhabited Mauritius and became extinct in the 17th century.

Replica of a Megalodon: A replica of the Megalodon, one of the largest sharks to ever exist, providing an impressive insight into the size and power of this prehistoric marine predator.

Replica of a saber-toothed tiger: Figure of the saber-toothed tiger animal that embodies the ferocity and power of this ancient predator.
